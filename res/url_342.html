    New Batch of Clinton-Era Papers Is Released

    By The New York Times

    The National Archives on Friday unsealed about 1,000 pages of documents from the Clinton administration, the sixth such release in recent months of memos, transcripts and speeches that had been withheld from public view.

    The William J. Clinton Presidential Library in Little Rock, Ark., released the documents on its website at 1 p.m. Eastern time.

    About 20,000 documents have been released since February. Highlights of those releases included Mr. Clinton complaining about the length of presidential speeches; details of tensions between the offices of Mr. Clinton and Mr. Gore; Mrs. Clintons struggles to define her political identity in the years before she became a senator, presidential candidate and secretary of state; and the administrations scrambles after Republicans won control of the House in 1994 and after the Monica Lewinsky scandal began to envelop the White House in 1998.

    Times reporters and editors are scouring the documents and will post updates on what we find.

    The remaining 13,000 or so previously undisclosed documents are to be released in the coming weeks.
